About the Brand

Global Bali Home is an international real estate company focused on the development of high-quality projects in Bali — one of the world's most attractive property markets. Drawing on global expertise in architecture, construction, and investment, we create projects defined by strong concepts and long-term value.

Each development is delivered in line with international standards, combining solid investment potential with considered, refined design. Our approach goes beyond aesthetics: we create spaces designed for both comfortable living and sustainable income, with efficient layouts, high construction quality, and a long-term perspective.

Global Bali Home brings together the character of Bali and international expertise through a strategic, forward-looking approach to real estate.

Tone of Voice

How Global Bali Home sounds in every channel — from a sales call to an Instagram caption. Four attributes define the voice; combine them, don't pick one. Draft, refine with team.

Voice Attributes

Grounded

We speak in numbers, contracts, and verifiable facts. No empty superlatives.

We are confident, not boastful.
Considered

Every sentence earns its place. We respect the reader's time and intelligence.

We are precise, not curt.
Transparent

Full legality is our USP. We disclose ownership, leasehold terms, fees, and risks upfront.

We are honest, not naive.
Calm

Bali is nature, breath, perspective. We never pressure. Urgency comes from real facts, not artificial scarcity.

We are inviting, not pushy.

We Say / We Don't Say

We say

"Leasehold: 29 years initial + 30-year extension under signed PPJB."

We don't say

"59-year ownership!" (misleading — extension is conditional)

"Investment developer focused on Bali villas, legal title and managed rental income."

"Luxury villa builder in Bali paradise!" (empty superlatives, no positioning)

"Available units in Wanayu — 3 out of 12. Handover Q4 2026."

"Hurry, only a few left!" (artificial scarcity)

"PT-PMA structure, taxes filed quarterly, all permits in your name."

"Don't worry about the legal stuff — we handle it." (opaque, undermines trust)

Language Rules — EN ↔ RU

  • RU: brand names stay in Latin script — Global Bali Home, Serenity Villas, Wanayu. Never transliterate ("Глобал Бали Хоум" is wrong).
  • RU: no anglicisms when a normal Russian word exists. "эстейт", "single-family", "драйвер", "офер" → use plain Russian equivalents.
  • RU: no calques like "коридор Убуда". Use "в Убуде", "в районе Убуда", or the specific village name.
  • Positioning: "international investment developer in Bali" / "международный инвестиционный девелопер на Бали". Not "villa builder", not "tied to Ubud".
  • Pricing: every USD figure must be accompanied by IDR (Indonesian law UU 7/2011). On the site IDR is primary, USD is auxiliary reference.

By Channel

Website / Landings

Full register. Facts first, emotion as flavour. Numbers in headlines beat adjectives.

Email / WhatsApp

Conversational but still precise. Use the client's name. Short paragraphs. No exclamation marks.

Instagram / Telegram

Shorter, more visual. Lead with one strong line. Lowercase project names allowed in headlines.

Paid Ads

One claim, one number, one CTA. Never "click here". Specify the action — "See available units", "Download leasehold guide".

Incorrect Usage

When using the logo, all applications must follow these guidelines:

  • 01 The logo must not be distorted or resized in a way that alters its proportions.
  • 02 Only the approved brand colors may be used.
  • 03 The structure and order of the logo elements must remain unchanged.
  • 04 The logo should only be placed on sufficiently contrasting brand backgrounds and must not be used on visually busy or highly detailed photographs.

Photography & Imagery

The brand's photography conveys a calm, nature-driven Bali lifestyle, focusing on warmth, atmosphere, and way of life rather than overt luxury.

Do

  • Use natural light, soft daylight, gentle diffused illumination.
  • Keep compositions open and balanced with clear horizontals.
  • Focus on architecture, nature, and surrounding environment.
  • Maintain a consistent visual rhythm and high-quality standards.
  • License all stock images legally before use.

Don't

  • Harsh shadows or high-contrast studio lighting.
  • Staged or exaggerated emotions in stock photos.
  • Heavy retouching or HDR effects.
  • Overly promotional or aggressive imagery.
  • Tight close-up details over wider environmental shots.

Margins

A consistent margin of 80px is applied across all standard layout formats. For print, A4 (210×297mm) uses a 30mm margin. These provide breathing room and maintain visual rhythm across formats.
